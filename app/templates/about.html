1. Executive Overview

The Human Development Index (HDI) was created by the UNDP to emphasize that people and their capabilities should be the ultimate criteria for assessing the development of a country, not economic growth alone. This project builds a highly optimized predictive model using historical data to automatically calculate HDI profiles from core health, education, and economic indicators.

2. Dataset Schema & Dictionary
Category Feature Name Data Type Description
Health Life Expectancy Continuous Average life expectancy at birth.
Education Expected Schooling Continuous Expected years of schooling for children.
Education Mean Schooling Continuous Mean years of schooling for adults.
Economy GNI Per Capita Continuous Gross National Income (PPP $).
3. Preprocessing & Feature Engineering
  • Missing Value Imputation: Dropped incomplete historical records using deterministic constraints to ensure highest quality training samples.
  • Income Normalization: Applied a natural logarithmic transformation (`np.log()`) to Gross National Income to correct for exponential skewness present in real-world wealth distributions.
  • Target Labels: The HDI target is synthesized based on geometric means. No standard scaling was required since Linear Regression natively learns the optimal coefficients for unscaled components.
